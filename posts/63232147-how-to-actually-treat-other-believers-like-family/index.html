    How to Actually Treat Other Believers Like Family

    March 9, 2024 · 3 min

    • 5. Encourage one another's spiritual growth

    As a Christian, we're called to treat fellow believers like family. That’s one of the ways we build a strong and cohesive spiritual community. However, sometimes we struggle with this ideal. We may have tensions or conflicts with other church members or just feel less connected than we should. It doesn't mean you've failed or your faith is waning, but it could be time for some introspection and reevaluation of your practices. Here are practical steps to treating other believers as family:

    1. Foster connections, not just attend events

    Being part of a religious community primarily through attending Sunday worship services is a good start, but it lacks the depth essential to family-like connection. Join or initiate small groups for studying and fellowship, get involved in volunteer activities, and don't forget to have fun together at recreational events too. When you spend more quality time with people, it fosters genuine bonds.

    2. Exercise empathy and openness

    When interacting with others, aim to see things from their perspective and show understanding (empathy). Also, practice open-mindedness — be genuinely interested in getting to know them, their stories, struggles, and victories. This opens up channels for true closeness and fosters a sense of family.

    3. Prioritize honest and respectful communication

    Regular and transparent dialogue is key to fostering any relationship, including those within the Church community. When issues arise, address them respectfully, openly, and humbly, following biblical principles. Offer forgiveness when appropriate and do not gossip or spread rumors about others. Honest communication breeds trust and family-like bonding.

    4. Share, give, and serve with each other

    Think of how a real family helps one another. Christians are called to live out this pattern by caring for and supporting each other. Take the initiative to reach out to struggling fellow members, offer financial help when needed, provide childcare during a crisis, or offer a helping hand with chores — the possibilities are endless! Model selflessness and grace in actions, as you would in a close-knit family.

    5. Encourage one another's spiritual growth

    A family does not leave one another to their own devices; they provide support and advice for personal growth. In the same way, help one another grow spiritually by reading Scriptures together, praying for one another, encouraging each other to live out their faith boldly, and sharing insights from personal Bible studies. Share the joys and the struggles of our walk as believers.

    6. Celebrate the milestones together

    Milestone markers, like baptisms, marriages, birthdays, anniversaries, and the arrival of new members, can become meaningful moments to draw closer as a group. Organize or participate in celebratory events where you can reflect on the spiritual journey of the individual and the entire church community. The bond that develops in these shared joyous moments will further bring you together.

    As you practice these steps, remember the key is your heart attitude first. Cultivate a mindset of love and unity. Pray for God to work in your heart and the hearts of the whole congregation for stronger ties and a more familial connection.
